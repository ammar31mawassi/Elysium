# Naming Directions

Dalili is now a working title. The new name should welcome students who use English, Hebrew, or Arabic without making one community feel like a guest in another community's product.

This is a naming shortlist, not legal trademark clearance. Search results and domain availability must be checked again immediately before choosing a name.

## Brand Character

Primary archetype: **Sage**.

- Clear, knowledgeable, calm, and precise.
- Helps students understand systems without talking down to them.

Secondary archetype: **Caregiver**.

- Supportive, human, and reassuring.
- Reduces shame and connects students to people.

The name should not sound like a tutoring marketplace, government office, generic AI assistant, or productivity tracker.

## Naming Criteria

A strong name should:

- Be pronounceable by English, Hebrew, and Arabic speakers.
- Work visually in Latin, Hebrew, and Arabic scripts.
- Avoid belonging clearly to only one language unless the concept itself is shared.
- Suggest place, direction, progress, connection, or access.
- Be short enough for an app icon and spoken recommendation.
- Avoid political, religious, or institutional ownership signals.
- Have a realistic chance of brand and domain distinction.

## Recommended Shortlist

### 1. MAQOM

Concept:

- A deliberately shared spelling inspired by Hebrew `makom` (מקום, place) and Arabic `maqam` (مقام, place/position/station), which are documented as cognates.
- Product story: find your place, understand where you are, and know where to go next.

Strengths:

- Cross-language idea without simply choosing one language.
- Distinctive Latin spelling.
- Fits the Campus Compass direction and belonging mission.
- Can support a strong line: "Your place on campus."

Risks:

- Users may pronounce it differently.
- The Arabic and Hebrew source words are related in idea but not identical in everyday use.
- Needs native-speaker testing before selection.

Current verdict: **strongest concept candidate**.

### 2. DARGA

Concept:

- Hebrew `darga` (דרגה) means rank or level; Arabic `daraja` (درجة) means degree, grade, level, or step.
- Product story: the next step in university life.

Strengths:

- Academic meaning exists naturally in both languages.
- Short, clear, and action-oriented.
- Supports visual ideas based on steps, levels, and progress without becoming a grade tracker.

Risks:

- The Arabic pronunciation is usually longer than the proposed brand spelling.
- "Level" can accidentally make the product feel performance-focused.

Current verdict: **strong practical candidate**.

### 3. SAHA

Concept:

- Arabic `saha` (ساحة) means a square, courtyard, or shared public space.
- The sound is short and pronounceable for Hebrew and English speakers, but this is an Arabic-rooted candidate rather than a genuinely shared word.
- Product story: the shared campus square where information and people meet.

Strengths:

- Warm, social, and easy to pronounce.
- Strong fit for groups, mentors, and belonging.
- Less formal than MAQOM or DARGA.

Risks:

- The word has many unrelated uses and brands.
- The slang meaning may feel too casual for official guidance.

Current verdict: **good community-led direction, weaker ownership**.

### 4. KAMPUS

Concept:

- A familiar transliteration of "campus" that can be written naturally as קמפוס and كامبوس.
- Product story: one student layer across the whole campus.

Strengths:

- Immediately understandable in all three languages.
- Neutral and easy to say.
- Does not require users to learn a new concept.

Risks:

- Highly generic and difficult to own.
- Likely to be crowded in domains, stores, and trademarks.

Current verdict: **excellent descriptor, weak standalone brand**.

### 5. MARKAZ

Concept:

- Arabic `markaz` (مركز) and Hebrew `merkaz` (מרכז) both mean center; modern Hebrew adopted the form through Arabic.
- Product story: the center of student life and practical support.

Strengths:

- Strongly recognizable across Arabic and Hebrew.
- Naturally communicates "hub."

Risks:

- Generic and widely used by organizations.
- The `a/e` pronunciation difference creates a spelling choice.

Current verdict: **meaningful but difficult to own**.

## Beautiful Meanings With Major Conflicts

These names are useful inspiration but should not lead the shortlist:

- **BAYT / BEIT**: shared word for home (بيت / בית), but `Bayt.com` is a major established platform and the term is widely used.
- **MIFTAH / MAFTEACH**: shared cognate for key (مفتاح / מפתח), but Miftaah is already used by major education organizations and MIFTAH has other prominent associations.
- **OFEQ / OFOQ**: horizon (אופק / أفق), but multiple active education platforms already use Ofoq.
- **YALLA**: widely understood in spoken Arabic and Hebrew, but extremely crowded and too casual for trusted academic guidance.
- **TANDEM**: communicates students helping one another, but it is already an established language-learning brand.
- **CAMPUSM / ONECAMPUS / UNIPATH**: descriptive but close to existing student-platform naming patterns and difficult to distinguish.

Preliminary conflict examples: [Bayt](https://www.bayt.com/), [Miftaah Institute](https://www.miftaah.org/), [Ofoq education platform](https://ofoqedu.net/), [campusM](https://campusm.exlibrisgroup.com/).

Linguistic references: [Hebrew מקום and Arabic مقام cognate note](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D7%9E%D7%A7%D7%95%D7%9D), [Klein Dictionary on Hebrew מרכז from Arabic markaz](https://www.sefaria.org/Klein_Dictionary%2C_%D7%9E%D6%B6%D7%A8%D6%B0%D7%9B%D6%B8%D6%BC%D7%96.1), [comparative Hebrew-Arabic lexicon including miftah/mafteach](https://seveleu.com/pages/semitic-syntax-morpho/comparative-sem).

## Suggested Naming Process

1. Keep **MAQOM**, **DARGA**, and **SAHA** as the first interview shortlist.
2. Ask at least five native speakers of each language to pronounce each name without coaching.
3. Ask what each name suggests before explaining the product.
4. Test the written forms in Latin, Hebrew, and Arabic scripts.
5. Reject any name that creates political, religious, or exclusionary associations for a meaningful group.
6. Run trademark, company, app-store, social-handle, and domain checks on the final two.
7. Select the name before creating production code, Base44 projects, or public branding.

## Working Recommendation

Use **MAQOM** as the leading exploration, **DARGA** as the clearest alternative, and **SAHA** as the community-oriented alternative.

Do not rename the GitHub repo yet. Rename only after native-speaker testing and a final decision.
